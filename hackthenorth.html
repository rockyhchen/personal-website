Canada's Largest Hackathon

Hack the North is an international, student-run, collegiate hackathon held at the University of Waterloo. A hackathon is an event students for teams around a problem or idea and collaboratively code a solution from scratch in just 36 hours. These projects can be made to address a particular social issue or they could just be something fun and challenging. Projects generally take the form of websites, mobile application or robots (hardware) but that's just the status quo.

User Personas

To approach the mobile app design, I lead the user persona discussion using GitHub. By first creating a template and then encouraging my team to discuss, we were able to rapid-develop detailed personas of hackers, sponsors, volunteers, press, judges and more. By describing real people with backgrounds, goals and values, we were able to uncover universal features and functionality.

Red Route Analysis

Once we knew what features we were going to build, there were issues in making this content consumable. How do we organize this information? How do we present said information to our users?

I performed a red route analysis to determine both the frequency and critical nature of the activities of our users (I know, it's blue to match the rest of this project - ironic). By interviewing our users, I was able to discover which features were more valuable. I used this information to determine our information hierarchy.

Proceeding the red route analysis, I was able to create a tab navigation that reflected my research. By utilizing a tab bar I was able to avoid the drawbacks of a hamburger button when it lumps all items together. Critical features were easy to see and access. Other features were placed under a modified hamburger button in the "more" tab.
